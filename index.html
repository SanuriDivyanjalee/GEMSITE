Gemstones

The world of gems is as diverse as it is fascinating, with jewels like glistening Diamonds, blazing Rubies, fiery Sapphires, and Tourmalines in all colors, Emeralds, dark red and green Garnets, blue Topaz, or yellow Citrines, and purple Amethysts. The four most well-known gems are known as the "Big Four": diamond, sapphire, emerald, and ruby. However, there are several additional gems that appeal to the heart, each of which is special in its own right, such as Alexandrite, Zircon, Peridot, Opal, Aquamarine, Spinel, and Tanzanite.

Gem Mining

Gemstones of exceptional quality and diversity have been prized in Sri Lanka for more than 2,500 years, and the nation still considers them to be its most valuable mineral resource. The practice of gemstone mining in Sri Lanka dates back to the time of the early monarchs.The Ratnapura district of the Sabaragamuwa province, which includes the communities of Eheliyagoda, Kuruwita, Ratnapura, Pelmadulla, Balangoda, Kalawana, and Rakwana, is the island's best-known and most productive gem-producing region.

Gem Jewellery

A gemstone must have the following qualities: durability, rarity, and beauty. Each and every item of jewelry is enhanced with colored gemstones.There are several rare gems that exhibit breath-taking phenomena including the cat's eye effect, star effect, opalescence, color play, and more. These stunning and priceless stones are the subject of numerous myths and stories, and many people hold them to have magical and healing properties.Sri Lanka is one of the the world's most reliable producers of premium Ceylon Sapphires and 75 other varieties of gemstones.
